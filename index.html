The Problem

Most teams don't fail from lack of effort. They fail against invisible friction.

When change initiatives stall, communication breaks down, or talented people leave — the instinct is to work harder, hire better, or restructure. But the real culprit is almost always the Friction Tax: the psychological and systemic hurdles silently draining energy, eroding trust, and stalling progress.

What makes our approach different: we give individuals and leaders the tools, frameworks, and responsibility to identify and solve their own workplace frictions — not just top-down mandates, but real ownership from every level of the organization.
